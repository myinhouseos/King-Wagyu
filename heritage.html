The King Wagyu Story

A Family Decision That Changed Everything

King Wagyu began with a simple conviction: that the most extraordinary beef in the world deserved to be raised with extraordinary care. In 2018, the Kars and Nienaber families established their Wagyu operation at Farm Houmoed in Hochfeld, Namibia — a decision grounded in both passion and precision.

Namibia's vast, clean landscapes and its strict veterinary standards make it one of the world's most compelling locations for premium beef production. The country's unique biosecurity environment, combined with Hochfeld's natural grazing conditions, provides a foundation that few operations anywhere can match.

We did not stumble into Wagyu. We chose it deliberately — researching genetics, studying the Japanese tradition, and building a programme from the ground up that honours everything that makes Wagyu exceptional.

Today, King Wagyu maintains a closed fullblood herd of Japanese Black cattle, an Akaushi up-breeding programme, and a commercial herd — all raised on the same farm, under the same philosophy of care, consistency, and genetic excellence.

Farm Houmoed

Hochfeld, Namibia

Situated in the Otjozondjupa region of central Namibia, Farm Houmoed sits in a landscape shaped by open plains, clean air, and an agricultural tradition that stretches back generations.

Namibia's biosecurity status is among the highest in Africa — foot-and-mouth disease free, with strict movement controls and veterinary oversight that meets European Union import requirements. For a premium beef operation, this is not a small detail. It is everything.

How We Farm

Our Guiding Philosophy

01

Genetics First

Every animal on this farm traces to documented, high-performance Japanese Black bloodlines. We invest in the best genetics available and manage them with discipline and long-term thinking.

02

Closed Herd Integrity

We raise all our Wagyu from birth to finish on Farm Houmoed. A closed herd gives us complete control over health, welfare, and the genetic consistency our customers depend on.

03

Patient Feeding

Premium marbling cannot be rushed. Our dedicated feedlot programme allows cattle to reach full marbling potential on a carefully balanced diet, over a timeline that prioritises quality over speed.

04

Low Stress Handling

Wagyu are sensitive animals and respond strongly to their environment. We apply low-stress handling techniques throughout — from paddock to feedlot — because calm cattle marble better and taste better.

05

Environmental Respect

We farm in harmony with the Namibian landscape. Sustainable grazing management protects the land for future generations while producing beef we are proud to put our name on.

06

Transparency

We maintain full traceability from birth to dispatch. Our customers — whether restaurants, retailers, or export partners — know exactly what they are buying and where it came from.

Our Journey

From Founding to Europe

2018

King Wagyu Founded

The Kars and Nienaber families establish King Wagyu at Farm Houmoed, Hochfeld. First fullblood Japanese Black genetics are imported and the foundation herd is assembled.

2019

Embryo Programme Launched

A structured embryo transfer and AI programme is established, accelerating genetic improvement and herd growth using elite international bloodlines.

2021

First Wagyu Beef Produced

The first King Wagyu animals complete the feedlot programme and are processed. Marbling grades confirm that the genetics and feeding protocol are delivering results.

2023

European Export Programme

King Wagyu achieves compliance with EU veterinary import requirements and begins supplying the European market with premium fullblood Wagyu beef.

Today

Growing. Improving. Consistently.

A closed fullblood herd, an active embryo programme, bull sales to breeders, and a growing export footprint. King Wagyu continues to build on a foundation of genetic integrity and family commitment.
