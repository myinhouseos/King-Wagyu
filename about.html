About King Wagyu

A family operation.
Built to last.

King Wagyu is a Fullblood Wagyu genetics, embryo, and export operation based on Farm Houmoed in the Otjozondjupa region of Namibia. Founded by the Kars family and operated by the Nienanbers, it was built by people who made deliberate choices to do this — not inherited convenience.

The Founding Story

How King Wagyu came to be

King Wagyu began in 2018 when 75 surrogate cows on Farm Houmoed were implanted with Fullblood Black Wagyu embryos — sired by some of the most respected Japanese bloodlines in the world. That was not a pilot program. It was a commitment.

The operation was founded by Dr. Alfred Adriaan (Fred) Kars — a man with a Doctorate in Agriculture and an MBA in Agri-business — who had spent his career understanding what productive, well-managed farming looks like. The decision to build a Fullblood Wagyu stud in Namibia, starting from foundation genetics, was not an accident. It was the conclusion of that career of knowledge.

"Top genetics fit for Kings — because the bloodlines we started with set the ceiling for everything that follows."

Deja Nienaber, Dr. Kars's daughter, took on the role of CEO and Owner — bringing with her a background as a registered Industrial Psychologist and a career spent in high-performance business environments. She exchanged that corporate world for the Wagyu value chain, and has since become one of the most credentialed voices in the Namibian and Southern African Wagyu industry.

Day-to-day operations on Farm Houmoed are run by DeBruin and Bianca Nienaber, who live on the property and ensure that every protocol designed for the program is executed on the ground, every day.

Foundation Genetics

Where the bloodlines come from

King Wagyu's herd was established from Fullblood Black Wagyu embryos sired by bloodlines that represent the highest expressions of the breed. These are not crossbreeding choices or commercial compromises — they are the sires that define premium Wagyu production internationally.

The founding sire lineup includes some of the most widely respected names in the Wagyu stud world. Their influence on marbling intensity, fat distribution, and eating quality is documented across thousands of progeny in Japan and internationally. King Wagyu's herd carries that foundation forward in Namibia.

Industry Affiliations & Credentials

Namibia Wagyu Breeders Society

Deja Nienaber serves as Vice-Chairlady. The Society was founded in 2017 — the same year the first Wagyu cattle were imported to Namibia — and King Wagyu has been part of its development from the outset.

World Wagyu Council

Deja sits on the Marketing Committee of the World Wagyu Council, the international body that connects Wagyu producers, industry bodies, and market participants across the globe.

Desert Wagyu

Deja is a founding board member of Desert Wagyu — the Namibian branded Wagyu beef product endorsed by the Namibian Meat Board and certified by Wagyu South Africa.

Protea Reserve Wagyu Co

A founding member of Protea Reserve Wagyu Co, a CWB-marketing cooperative supporting Southern African Wagyu producers with route-to-market access and commercial infrastructure.

Wagyu South Africa

King Wagyu participates in the Wagyu South Africa certification framework, with a registered footprint of 47 Fullblood animals and 32 surrogates in South Africa alongside the Namibia operation.

Wagyu Breeders Society of Namibia

An associate member of the World Wagyu Council, the Society links Namibian Wagyu producers to the global industry. King Wagyu was involved in the Namibian industry from its formation in 2017.
