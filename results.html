Embryo Program

Flush yields, conception rates, and calf outcomes

The embryo program at King Wagyu is measured against the inputs it requires and the outputs it delivers. Donor management, recipient quality, and synchronisation discipline are all tracked — because these are what determine whether the program is commercially viable, not just biologically possible.

The figures below reflect program performance with managed recipient herds under King Wagyu protocols. Conception rates vary by recipient condition, transfer timing, and seasonal factors — the ranges shown represent outcomes across multiple flush cycles.

What each phase of the program delivers

The King Wagyu production system is measured at each phase — not only at the point of slaughter or export. These are the targets and outcomes across the full production sequence.

Phase 01

Genetics & Breeding

Sires selected against top-percentile EBVs for marbling and structure. Donor cows selected from animals with proven progeny performance.

Outcome Target High-EBV Fullblood progeny, verified parentage
Phase 02

Embryo & Calf Crop

Export Performance

What reaches the buyer is what was documented at birth

"The pedigree is not an administrative formality. It is the proof of the product."

King Wagyu animals destined for live export or export processing carry the same documentation chain from birth: pedigree registration, DNA parentage verification, health records, and production history. This is not assembled on request — it is maintained as a matter of operational standard.

For live export buyers — whether purchasing breeding bulls, donor cows, or weaners — the documentation record allows classification verification, pedigree assessment, and health compliance checking before arrival. There are no gaps to reconstruct.

For processed beef export, carcass data including grade, yield, and health certification is linked to the same animal record. Buyers receive traceability from sire selection to hook weight — which is increasingly a requirement rather than a differentiator in premium export markets.

A Note on Results

What we will and won't claim

King Wagyu is an operating program, not a marketing position. The figures on this page reflect what we target and what we measure — not the best single result extracted from a dataset and presented as representative.

Production data in Wagyu is inherently variable. Weather, recipient condition, individual animal responses, and seasonal feed quality all affect outcomes that are otherwise well-controlled. We do not hide that variability — we manage it, track it, and use it to improve the program across each cycle.
