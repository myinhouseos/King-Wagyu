The Breed

Japanese Black — The Gold Standard

Not all Wagyu is created equal. King Wagyu raises exclusively fullblood Japanese Black cattle — the breed behind the world's most celebrated beef. Japanese Black (Kuroge Washu) is genetically predisposed to produce extraordinary intramuscular fat, creating the rich marbling that defines true Wagyu flavour.

We source genetics from proven international bloodlines, importing embryos and semen that represent the top of the breed. Every animal on our farm carries documented, registered ancestry — giving our customers full confidence in what they are buying.

The difference between Wagyu and authentic fullblood Wagyu is not subtle. It is measurable, visible in every slice, and unmistakable on the palate.

Understanding Marbling

The Beef Marbling Standard

Wagyu beef is graded using the Beef Marbling Standard (BMS), a Japanese scoring system from 1 to 12. While commodity beef rarely exceeds BMS 3, King Wagyu targets BMS 7 and above — a level of intramuscular fat that delivers exceptional tenderness, depth of flavour, and the distinctive richness Wagyu is famous for.

1–2 Standard
3–4 Choice
5–6 Premium
7–9 King Wagyu
10–12 Exceptional
Flavour

Umami depth unlike any other beef

The intramuscular fat in genuine Wagyu does not simply add richness — it changes the nature of flavour entirely. At BMS 7+, each bite carries layers of umami, sweetness, and a lingering finish that commodity beef cannot replicate.

Tenderness

Melts at lower temperatures

Wagyu fat has a lower melting point than other beef fats, meaning it literally melts in the mouth at body temperature. The result is a texture that is simultaneously rich and delicate — an experience that has made Wagyu the world's most sought-after beef.

Health

Higher in mono-unsaturated fat

Wagyu beef contains significantly higher levels of oleic acid — the same mono-unsaturated fatty acid found in olive oil — compared to conventional beef. This gives it a nutritional profile that sets it apart from other red meats.

Consistency

Genetics guarantee results

The extraordinary marbling in our beef is not luck — it is the outcome of deliberate genetic selection over years. By controlling genetics and feeding protocol within our closed herd, we deliver consistent marbling grades that our customers can depend on.

How We Raise Our Cattle

Ethical Farming, Exceptional Results

Premium Wagyu cannot be produced under stress. Our farming methods prioritise animal welfare, environmental sustainability, and the conditions that allow cattle to reach their full genetic potential.

01

Closed Herd

All animals are born and raised on Farm Houmoed. We introduce no outside cattle — protecting biosecurity and maintaining complete genetic and health traceability.

02

Natural Grazing

Our cattle spend their early lives on Namibia's natural veld, developing on pasture before entering the structured feedlot programme. Clean land, clean air, clean animals.

03

Dedicated Feedlot

Finishing is done on our own feedlot using a carefully formulated diet. We do not rush the feeding period — superior marbling requires patience and precision.

04

Low-Stress Handling

Wagyu respond acutely to stress, and stress affects marbling. Our handlers are trained in low-stress techniques — quiet yards, calm movements, and minimal separation anxiety.
